NELSON MANDELA

Long Walk To Freedom

HERE IS A BRIEF OUTLINE OF THE LIFE OF NELSON MANDELA

  • Rolihlahla Mandela was born into the Madiba clan in the village of Mvezo, in the Eastern Cape, on 18 July 1918. His mother was Nonqaphi Nosekeni and his father was Nkosi Mphakanyiswa Gadla Mandela
  • He attended primary school in Qunu, where he was given the name Nelson as per the custom of giving children "Christian" names.
  • After finishing secondary school at Healdtown, Mandela enrolled for B.A Degree at Fort Hare University.
  • Mandela was expelled at Fort Hare for participating in a student protest.
  • In 1941, while working as a security officer, he met Walter Sisulu.
  • He completed his BA through the University of South Africa and went back to Fort Hare for his graduation in 1943.Mandela, while increasingly politically involved from 1942, only joined the African National Congress in 1944 when he helped to form the ANC Youth League (ANCYL).
  • Mandela rose through the ranks of the ANCYL and through its efforts, the ANC adopted a more radical mass-based policy, the Programme of Action, in 1949.
  • August 1952 he and Oliver Tambo established South Africa’s first black law firm, Mandela & Tambo.
  • 11 June 1964 Mandela was sentenced to inprisonmnent for charges of treason.
  • He was released from prison in 1990On 10 May 1994 he was inaugurated as South Africa’s first democratically elected President. On his 80th birthday in 1998 he married Graça Machel, his third wife
  • He died at his home in Johannesburg on 5 December 2013.
